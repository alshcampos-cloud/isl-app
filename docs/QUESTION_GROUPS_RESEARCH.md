## 1. RECOMMENDED QUESTION GROUPS

### Group 1: First Impressions (The Opener Questions)
**Description:** The 5-8 questions that open nearly every interview. These are the highest-frequency, highest-stakes questions because they set the interviewer's first impression and anchor the entire conversation.

**Why it exists:** Every prep app has these, but most treat them as throwaway warmups. In reality, a weak opener tanks the whole interview. These deserve dedicated, deep practice.

**Default:** ON

**Sample Questions:**
1. Tell me about yourself.
2. Why are you interested in this role?
3. What do you know about our company?
4. Walk me through your resume.
5. Why are you leaving your current position?
6. What are you looking for in your next role?
7. How did you hear about this position?
8. What would you want to accomplish in your first 90 days?

---

### Group 2: Behavioral Foundations (Core STAR Stories)
**Description:** The classic behavioral questions that assess past performance as a predictor of future behavior. These are the backbone of structured interviews at every major company. Each question maps to a core competency: teamwork, conflict, leadership, initiative, adaptability.

**Why it exists:** Companies like Amazon, Google, and McKinsey spend 60%+ of interview time on behavioral questions. Having 5-7 strong STAR stories that can flex across these questions is the single most important prep activity.

**Default:** ON

**Sample Questions:**
1. Tell me about a time you had a conflict with a coworker. How did you resolve it?
2. Describe a situation where you had to lead a team through a difficult project.
3. Give me an example of a time you failed. What did you learn?
4. Tell me about a time you had to work with someone whose personality was very different from yours.
5. Describe a situation where you had to prioritize competing demands under a tight deadline.
6. Tell me about a time you went above and beyond what was expected.
7. Give an example of when you had to persuade someone to see things your way.
8. Tell me about a time you received critical feedback. How did you respond?

---

### Group 3: Situational Problem-Solving
**Description:** Hypothetical "what would you do if..." questions that test real-time reasoning, judgment, and decision-making. Unlike behavioral questions (past tense), these are future tense and require candidates to think out loud.

**Why it exists:** Situational questions reveal how someone thinks, not just what they have done. They test analytical ability, values, and composure under ambiguity. Google's GCA (General Cognitive Ability) assessment leans heavily on these.

**Default:** ON

**Sample Questions:**
1. What would you do if you disagreed with your manager's decision on a project?
2. How would you handle a situation where a teammate consistently missed deadlines?
3. If you were assigned a project in an area you have no experience in, how would you approach it?
4. What would you do if you realized you made a significant mistake on a deliverable that already shipped?
5. How would you handle a situation where two of your priorities directly conflict?
6. If a client made an unreasonable request, how would you respond?
7. What would you do if you noticed a colleague behaving unethically?

---

### Group 4: Self-Awareness and Vulnerability
**Description:** Questions designed to test whether a candidate can be honest, reflective, and genuinely self-aware rather than performing rehearsed humility. These probe for authentic vulnerability, not scripted weakness answers.

**Why it exists:** Eric's concept of "tell me about a time something bad happened" is powerful because most candidates over-rehearse weakness questions into disguised strengths. This group trains candidates to show genuine reflection without self-sabotage. Interviewers increasingly value authenticity over polish. Emotional intelligence research shows self-awareness is the strongest predictor of leadership effectiveness.

**Default:** ON

**Sample Questions:**
1. Tell me about a time something went really wrong at work. What was your role in it?
2. What is the hardest piece of feedback you have ever received? How did it change you?
3. What is one thing you are actively working to improve about yourself right now?
4. Describe a time you realized you were wrong about something important. What did you do?
5. What would your biggest critic say about working with you?
6. Tell me about a time your ego got in the way. What happened?
7. What professional experience are you least proud of? What did you take from it?
8. If I called your last manager right now, what would they say is your biggest area for growth?

---

### Group 5: Communication and Relationship Building
**Description:** Questions that assess how candidates build rapport, communicate across differences, influence without authority, and navigate interpersonal dynamics. These test active listening, empathy, and collaborative communication.

**Why it exists:** Communication skills consistently rank as the top hiring criteria across industries. These questions specifically test the skill that InterviewAnswers.AI helps users develop, making them a natural fit for deep practice with delivery feedback.

**Default:** ON

**Sample Questions:**
1. Tell me about a time you had to explain something complex to someone with no background in the topic.
2. Describe a situation where miscommunication caused a problem. How did you fix it?
3. Give an example of a time you had to deliver bad news to a stakeholder.
4. Tell me about a time you had to build a relationship with someone who was initially resistant.
5. Describe how you handle giving feedback to someone who is more senior than you.
6. Tell me about a time active listening changed the outcome of a situation.
7. How do you adjust your communication style for different audiences?

---

### Group 6: Leadership and Influence
**Description:** Questions that probe leadership beyond formal authority, including leading peers, driving change from below, making tough calls, and developing others. Maps directly to Amazon's "Ownership" and "Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit" principles.

**Why it exists:** Leadership questions appear at every level, not just management roles. Companies like Amazon expect every employee to demonstrate ownership. These questions also surface strategic thinking and decision-making quality.

**Default:** ON

**Sample Questions:**
1. Tell me about a time you led a project without formal authority.
2. Describe a decision you made that was unpopular. How did you handle it?
3. Give an example of when you mentored or developed someone on your team.
4. Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information.
5. Describe a situation where you had to get buy-in from multiple stakeholders with competing interests.
6. Tell me about a time you identified a problem no one else saw and took initiative to fix it.
7. How do you handle a situation where you disagree with your team's consensus?

---

### Group 7: Adaptability and Resilience
**Description:** Questions about handling change, ambiguity, setbacks, and unfamiliar territory. Tests growth mindset, emotional resilience, and comfort with uncertainty.

**Why it exists:** Post-pandemic, adaptability has become the most valued soft skill. Google specifically evaluates "dealing with ambiguity" as a core competency. These questions also address the "culture fit" dimension that many companies weigh heavily.

**Default:** ON

**Sample Questions:**
1. Tell me about a time you had to adapt to a major change at work. How did you handle it?
2. Describe a situation where you had to learn something completely new very quickly.
3. Tell me about a time a project you were working on was suddenly deprioritized or cancelled.
4. Give an example of how you handled a period of significant uncertainty at work.
5. Tell me about your biggest professional setback. How did you recover?
6. Describe a time you thrived outside your comfort zone.

---

### Group 8: Curveball and Creative Thinking
**Description:** Unexpected, off-script questions designed to test composure, creative thinking, personality, and the ability to think on your feet. These range from philosophical ("What would you do with a million dollars?") to analytical ("How many gas stations are in the US?") to personality-revealing ("What is the last thing you learned for fun?").

**Why it exists:** These questions deliberately break candidates out of rehearsed mode. The interviewer is evaluating process, not answer, and the ability to stay composed when surprised. This is where Think Faster, Talk Smarter training creates the biggest competitive advantage.

**Default:** ON

**Sample Questions:**
1. If you could have dinner with any three people, living or dead, who would you choose and why?
2. What is the most interesting thing about you that is not on your resume?
3. If you were an animal, which one would you be and why?
4. Explain something you are passionate about as if I know nothing about it.
5. What would you do if you won the lottery tomorrow?
6. What is the last thing you changed your mind about?
7. If you had to teach a class on any topic, what would it be?
8. What is a hill you would die on professionally?

---

### Group 9: Values and Culture Fit
**Description:** Questions that probe alignment between the candidate's values and the company's culture. Tests intrinsic motivation, work style preferences, and what the candidate cares about beyond compensation.

**Why it exists:** Google's "Googleyness" factor, Amazon's "Hire and Develop the Best," and most modern hiring rubrics include a culture/values dimension. These questions are deceptively simple but reveal deep alignment or misalignment.

**Default:** ON (subset); full group opt-in

**Sample Questions:**
1. What kind of work environment do you do your best work in?
2. What motivates you beyond a paycheck?
3. How do you define success in your career?
4. Tell me about a company value at your last job that you genuinely believed in. Tell me about one you struggled with.
5. What do you do when your personal values conflict with a company decision?
6. How do you want your colleagues to describe you?
7. What is the most important quality you look for in a manager?

### Group 10: Spontaneous Speaking Drills (Think Faster, Talk Smarter)
**Description:** A UNIQUE category that no competitor offers. These are not traditional interview questions. They are structured improvisation exercises based on Matt Abrahams' frameworks from Stanford. Each drill practices a specific communication structure (What-So What-Now What, Problem-Solution-Benefit, PREP, ADD) in an interview-relevant context.

**Why it exists:** This is InterviewAnswers.AI's differentiation. Traditional prep apps give you questions to rehearse. We train the underlying SKILL of spontaneous, structured communication. Candidates who can structure thoughts on the fly will outperform candidates who memorize answers, especially on follow-up questions and curveball moments.

**Default:** ON (introductory drills); advanced drills opt-in

**Drill Format:** Each drill specifies a structure, gives a prompt, and the AI coaches the candidate on using the structure in real-time.

**Sample Drills:**

1. **What-So What-Now What Drill:** "Your interviewer just said: 'We are restructuring the team next quarter.' Respond using What-So What-Now What." (Trains: processing new information and responding with structure)

2. **Problem-Solution-Benefit Drill:** "Pitch a process improvement you made at a past job using Problem-Solution-Benefit format." (Trains: persuasive storytelling)

3. **PREP Drill (Point-Reason-Example-Point):** "The interviewer asks: 'Why should we hire you over other candidates?' Answer using PREP." (Trains: concise, high-impact answers)

4. **ADD Drill (Answer-Detail-Describe relevance):** "The interviewer asks a follow-up you did not expect: 'How do you handle situations where you have zero context?' Use ADD." (Trains: follow-up question handling)

5. **Anxiety Reframe Drill:** "You just blanked on a question. Practice the pause-breathe-structure recovery technique." (Trains: composure under pressure)

6. **Active Listening Drill:** "Listen to a 30-second interviewer monologue about their team's challenges. Paraphrase it back, then connect your experience. Use 'Yes, and...' improv technique." (Trains: listening, connecting, not just waiting to talk)

7. **60-Second Story Drill:** "Tell a complete STAR story about teamwork in under 60 seconds. The AI coaches on pacing, filler words, and completeness." (Trains: conciseness)

8. **Redirect Drill:** "The interviewer asks about a skill you do not have. Practice acknowledging the gap and bridging to a related strength using What-So What-Now What." (Trains: handling weakness questions authentically)

### Group 11: Amazon Leadership Principles
**Description:** Questions mapped to Amazon's 16 Leadership Principles. Amazon's behavioral interview process is considered the gold standard, and these principles map well to most corporate environments even outside Amazon.

**Why it exists:** Amazon hires hundreds of thousands of people using this framework. Many other companies have adopted similar competency models. Preparing for Amazon LPs trains transferable skills.

**Default:** OFF (opt-in). Surfaces when user indicates they are preparing for Amazon or tech company interviews.

**Sample Questions:**
1. **Customer Obsession:** Tell me about a time you had to choose between what was easy and what was right for the customer.
2. **Ownership:** Describe a time you took on something outside your area of responsibility because it needed to be done.
3. **Invent and Simplify:** Tell me about a time you found a simple solution to a seemingly complex problem.
4. **Bias for Action:** Describe a time you took a calculated risk. What was the outcome?
5. **Earn Trust:** Tell me about a time you had to rebuild trust with a colleague or stakeholder.
6. **Dive Deep:** Give an example of a time the data told a different story than what people assumed.
7. **Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit:** Tell me about a time you disagreed with a group decision but committed to it anyway.
8. **Deliver Results:** Describe the most challenging goal you achieved and how you did it.

**Description:** Clinical-context behavioral and situational questions for nursing and healthcare roles. Uses SBAR and STAR frameworks. All clinical content is human-validated and sourced from published frameworks (NCSBN Clinical Judgment Model, Nursing Process, Maslow's, ABC).

**Why it exists:** This is the nursing track within InterviewAnswers.AI. These questions are already built (70 Erin-approved questions in nursingQuestions.js). This group surfaces for users who select the nursing specialty.

**Default:** OFF for general users. AUTO-ON for nursing track users.

2. Describe a time you provided effective patient or family education.
3. Give an example of a time you had to interact with a hostile patient.

**Clinical Judgment:**
4. Describe a time you noticed a subtle change in a patient's condition that others missed.
5. Tell me about a time you had to prioritize between multiple critical patients.

**Teamwork and Communication:**
6. Tell me about a conflict within your healthcare team. How did you resolve it?
7. Describe how you communicate with physicians when you disagree with a treatment plan.

**Ethical Dilemmas:**
8. Tell me about a time you faced an ethical dilemma in patient care. How did you navigate it?

### Group 13: Technical and Role-Specific Competency
**Description:** A framework for industry-specific technical questions that assess domain knowledge, methodology, and practical skills. This group is a template that can be customized per industry vertical as InterviewAnswers.AI expands.

**Why it exists:** Behavioral questions test how you work. Technical questions test what you know. Both matter. This group acknowledges that gap without trying to be a technical study tool; instead it trains candidates to COMMUNICATE their technical knowledge clearly.

**Default:** OFF (opt-in per industry). Initially available as a framework, not pre-populated.

**Sample Questions (generic templates):**
1. Walk me through how you would approach [domain-specific problem].
2. What tools and methodologies do you use for [core responsibility]?
3. Describe your process for [key technical skill].
4. How do you stay current with developments in [your field]?
5. Tell me about a technical challenge you solved and how you explained it to a non-technical stakeholder.

### Group 14: Salary, Negotiation, and Closing
**Description:** The questions that come at the end of the interview or during the offer stage. These are high-stakes because candidates consistently underperform on negotiation and closing questions out of discomfort.

**Why it exists:** Most prep apps ignore this entirely. But "Do you have any questions for me?" and "What are your salary expectations?" are where interviews are won or lost. Training these builds confidence for the most anxiety-inducing moments.

**Default:** ON (subset of 4 key questions); full group opt-in

**Sample Questions:**
1. What are your salary expectations?
2. Do you have any other offers you are considering?
3. Why should we hire you?
4. Do you have any questions for us? (Train: asking smart questions)
5. What would make you turn down this offer?
6. When can you start?
7. Is there anything else you would like us to know?

### Group 15: Rapid-Fire Warmup Drills
**Description:** Short, timed exercises (30-60 seconds each) designed as warmups before a real interview. These are not full questions but micro-drills that activate the spontaneous speaking muscle. Based on Matt Abrahams' concept of "daring to be dull" (prioritizing connection over perfection) and improv "Yes, and..." techniques.

**Why it exists:** Athletes warm up before games. Musicians warm up before concerts. Interview candidates should warm up before interviews. No competitor offers this. It leverages InterviewAnswers.AI's real-time speech analysis to provide immediate delivery feedback.

**Default:** ON (surfaces as a pre-interview warmup option)

**Sample Drills:**
1. **30-Second Story:** Tell any story from your week in exactly 30 seconds. (Trains: pacing, conciseness)
2. **Explain It Simply:** Explain your job to a 10-year-old. (Trains: jargon removal, clarity)
3. **Three Things:** Name three things you are grateful for about your current or last job. Go. (Trains: positive framing, speed)
4. **Yes, And:** The AI says a statement about your hypothetical new job. Build on it with "Yes, and..." (Trains: active listening, improv, positivity)
5. **Pause Practice:** Answer "What do you do?" but pause for 2 full seconds before starting. (Trains: composure, eliminating filler words)
6. **Bridge Drill:** The AI gives you an off-topic question. Bridge back to your core message in one sentence. (Trains: redirection)

## 3. THINK FASTER, TALK SMARTER INTEGRATION

### Why This Is Our Moat

Every interview prep app gives users questions to practice. Nobody trains the underlying communication skill. Matt Abrahams' research from Stanford provides a framework for exactly this: structured spontaneous communication.

**What competitors do:** Give you a question. Let you record an answer. Maybe give you generic feedback like "good use of STAR method."

**What we do differently:** Teach you communication STRUCTURES that work in any spontaneous moment, then drill those structures with real-time delivery feedback (pacing, filler words, structure adherence, conciseness).

### The Six Abrahams Structures We Should Implement

| Structure | Components | Best For |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **What-So What-Now What** | State it, explain why it matters, describe next steps | Follow-up questions, unexpected pivots, "tell me more" moments |
| **Problem-Solution-Benefit** | Name the issue, present your solution, describe the outcome | Behavioral stories, achievement descriptions, case study answers |
| **PREP** | Point, Reason, Example, Point | Direct questions like "Why should we hire you?", "What is your greatest strength?" |
| **ADD** | Answer, Detailed example, Describe relevance | Q&A after presentations, follow-up probing questions |
| **Past-Present-Future** | Where you were, where you are, where you are going | "Tell me about yourself", career trajectory questions |
| **Comparison-Contrast** | Option A vs Option B with tradeoffs | Decision-making questions, "How would you approach X vs Y?" |

### How This Creates Unique Question Types

**Traditional question:** "Tell me about a time you handled a conflict."
**Our version:** Same question, but the AI coaches the candidate to use Problem-Solution-Benefit structure and gives real-time feedback on whether their answer hit all three components.

**Traditional question:** "Why should we hire you?"
**Our version:** Same question, but drilled using PREP structure. AI feedback specifically evaluates: Did you state your point? Give a reason? Provide an example? Restate your point?

**No competitor equivalent:** "The interviewer just told you the team is struggling with X. Respond using What-So What-Now What." This is pure spontaneous communication training. No other app does this.

### Anxiety Management (Abrahams' Top Contribution)

Abrahams' research identifies anxiety as the primary barrier to effective spontaneous communication. His techniques:

1. **Double exhale breathing** — Extended exhalation activates vagus nerve, reduces physical anxiety response
2. **Reframe anxiety as excitement** — Same physiological response, different cognitive label
3. **"Dare to be dull"** — Remove pressure to be brilliant; focus on connection over perfection
4. **Stockpiling** — Pre-prepare stories, facts, and anecdotes that can be pulled into any answer

**Product implication:** We should build a pre-interview "calm down" routine that includes breathing exercises and a quick warmup drill. Group 15 (Rapid-Fire Warmup) serves this purpose.

## 4. QUICK START SET (Interview in 24 Hours)

For a user with an interview tomorrow, overwhelming them with 100+ questions is counterproductive. Instead, focus on the highest-frequency, highest-impact questions.

### The 12-Question Emergency Prep Kit

**Must Practice (the 5 questions asked in 90%+ of interviews):**
1. Tell me about yourself. (Group 1)
2. Why are you interested in this role? (Group 1)
3. Tell me about a time you handled a conflict. (Group 2)
4. What is your greatest weakness? (Group 4)
5. Do you have any questions for us? (Group 14)

**Should Practice (asked in 60-80% of interviews):**
6. Why are you leaving your current position? (Group 1)
7. Tell me about a time you failed. (Group 2)
8. Describe a time you led a project. (Group 6)
9. What are your salary expectations? (Group 14)

**Bonus Round (build confidence, not just content):**
10. 30-Second Story warmup drill (Group 15)
11. Pause Practice warmup drill (Group 15)
12. One curveball question for composure practice (Group 8)

### Quick Start Mode Behavior
- Present these 12 questions in sequence
- After each answer, give brief targeted feedback (30 seconds max)
- Focus feedback on: structure (did they use STAR/PREP?), length (under 2 minutes?), and filler words
- End with a confidence score and 2-3 specific tips for tomorrow

## 5. DEEP PREP SET (Interview in 1 Week)

### Day-by-Day 7-Day Prep Plan

**Day 1: Foundation (Groups 1 + 2)**
- Practice all 8 opener questions
- Draft 5 core STAR stories that cover: conflict, leadership, failure, teamwork, initiative
- AI feedback focus: story structure and completeness

**Day 2: Self-Knowledge (Groups 4 + 9)**
- Practice vulnerability and self-awareness questions
- Practice culture fit questions
- AI feedback focus: authenticity, avoiding scripted humility

**Day 3: Problem-Solving and Adaptability (Groups 3 + 7)**
- Practice situational and adaptability questions
- AI feedback focus: structured thinking, composure with hypotheticals

**Day 4: Communication Structures (Group 10)**
- Learn and drill What-So What-Now What
- Learn and drill Problem-Solution-Benefit
- Learn and drill PREP
- AI feedback focus: structure adherence, conciseness

**Day 5: Leadership + Curveballs (Groups 6 + 8)**
- Practice leadership questions with advanced STAR
- Practice curveball questions using spontaneous speaking structures
- AI feedback focus: composure, creativity, personality showing through

**Day 6: Closing and Negotiation (Group 14)**
- Practice salary questions, closing questions
- Practice asking smart questions
- AI feedback focus: confidence, specificity, avoiding underselling

**Day 7: Full Mock Interview + Warmup Drills (Groups 10 + 15)**
- Run a full 30-minute mock interview pulling from all groups
- Do rapid-fire warmup drills
- Review areas for improvement
- AI feedback focus: overall impression, top 3 things to remember

## 6. WHAT MAKES A GREAT QUESTION BANK vs A GENERIC ONE

### Research Findings

| Feature | Generic Banks | Great Banks |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| Organization | Flat list, no progression | Grouped by competency with difficulty levels |
| Follow-ups | None | 2-3 follow-up chains per question that go deeper |
| Context | Just the question | Practice tips, common pitfalls, why interviewers ask this |
| Difficulty | All the same | Labeled (Foundation / Intermediate / Advanced) |
| Feedback | "Good job" or generic rubric | Structure-specific (did you use STAR? Was your result quantified?) |
| Adaptivity | Static list | Adapts based on performance (weak on conflict? Surface more conflict Qs) |
| Industry | Generic only | Core + industry-specific overlays |
| Delivery | Ignores how you say it | Coaches pacing, filler words, confidence, conciseness |

### Our Competitive Advantages

1. **Communication structure coaching** (Think Faster, Talk Smarter integration) — no competitor does this
2. **Real-time delivery feedback** (filler words, pacing, pauses) — a few competitors do basic versions
3. **Follow-up chains** — our questions include follow-ups that go 2-3 levels deep, training candidates for the probe that comes AFTER the rehearsed answer
4. **Warmup drills** — nobody offers pre-interview warmup exercises
5. **Difficulty progression** — questions labeled Foundation/Intermediate/Advanced so users can scale
6. **Vulnerability training** — dedicated group for authentic self-awareness, not scripted humility

### What We Should Build

1. **Every question needs follow-ups.** The first answer is rehearsed. The second and third follow-ups are where real skill shows. Our AI should automatically probe deeper after the initial answer.

2. **Every question needs a "why they ask this" tip.** Knowing the interviewer's intent helps candidates tailor their answers. Example: "They are not asking about your weakness. They are asking: Can you be self-aware without being self-destructive?"

3. **Difficulty labels matter.** New grads need different questions than senior leaders. Tag every question: Foundation (entry-level), Intermediate (mid-career), Advanced (senior/exec).

4. **Communication structure tags.** Tag each question with the recommended Abrahams structure: "Best answered with PREP" or "Use Problem-Solution-Benefit here." The AI can then coach structure adherence.

5. **Delivery metrics per question.** Track: average answer length, filler word frequency, structure adherence score, and confidence rating. Show users their improvement over time.
