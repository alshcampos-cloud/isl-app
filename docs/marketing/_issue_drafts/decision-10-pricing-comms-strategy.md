# Pricing communication strategy — when to announce $39 / $149 publicly?

**Labels suggestion:** marketing, decision, brand-canon, paid-spend, wave-3
**Assignees suggestion:** @lucas (owner) + @jacobdev-prog (A/B test design)

---

## Context

Current pricing is locked per `JACOB_FIXES_IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN_2026-05-08.md` #8a: $39 30-day pass, $19.99 nursing pass, $149 annual pass. **Not yet publicly communicated** in marketing creative — existing creative leads with the philosophy ("Practice, not cheat") and not the price. Question: whether/when/how to surface price prominently.

## Options

- **A) Lead with price in paid creative ("$39 for 30 days of practice")** — Trades on price clarity. Risk: commoditization. Competitors offer "free with real-time cheat assistance"; price-led messaging gets compared dollar-for-dollar.
- **B) Keep price out of creative entirely; reveal only on landing page** — Lead-gen funnel: creative sells philosophy, page reveals price. Trusts the wedge to qualify before price reveal.
- **C) Test both as paired ad variants** — Same hook, one with price, one without. Read CTR + conversion-rate differential. Cleanest empirical answer.
- **D) Lead with annual ($149) in creative** — Frames us as long-term tool, not interview-week panic buy. Higher AOV, smaller addressable market for lead message.

## Recommended

**C** as experiment design. Brand voice favors B; engineering rigor says test it. Run comparison once D1 (pixels) is resolved.

## Jacob's input requested

- Experiment design: same hook + two price variants — is that a clean A/B, or does the variant signal influence other ad-set decisions (audience, placement, time)?
- Statistical-significance threshold: what's the minimum spend to read a reliable differential?
- How to design so we don't burn the wedge if the "with price" variant performs better — i.e., what's the brand-voice guardrail?
