**Format:** 10 slides, 1080x1350px portrait
**Target:** Job seekers 22-35, anxious about upcoming interviews
**Caption:** You've Googled "how to answer tell me about yourself" at 2am. We've all been there. Here are 5 answers that actually land the job -- backed by the STAR method. Save this. You'll need it. Link in bio to practice with our AI Mock Interviewer.
**Hashtags:** #interviewtips #jobinterview #careeradvice #starmethod #interviewprep #jobsearch2026 #interviewanswers

### Slide 1 (Cover)
**Headline:** 5 Interview Answers That Actually Work
**Subtext:** With real examples you can steal
**Design note:** Bold typography, teal brand color background, slight texture

### Slide 2
**Headline:** "Tell me about yourself"
**Body:** They don't want your life story. Use this formula: Present (what you do now) + Past (relevant experience) + Future (why THIS role). Keep it under 90 seconds.
**Example framework:** "I'm currently a [role] at [company] where I [key achievement]. Before that, I [relevant background]. I'm excited about this role because [specific connection to the job]."

### Slide 3
**Headline:** "What's your greatest weakness?"
**Body:** Never say "I'm a perfectionist." Pick a REAL skill gap you've actively worked on. The interviewer wants self-awareness, not a humble brag.
**Example framework:** "I used to struggle with [real thing]. I noticed it was affecting [specific outcome], so I [concrete action you took]. Now I [measurable improvement]."

### Slide 4
**Headline:** "Tell me about a time you failed"
**Body:** This is a STAR method question in disguise. They want to see how you recover, not that you're perfect.
**Framework:** Situation (what happened) + Task (your role) + Action (what you did to fix it) + Result (what you learned and changed)

### Slide 5
**Headline:** "Why should we hire you?"
**Body:** Don't just list your skills. Connect YOUR specific strengths to THEIR specific problems. Research the company beforehand. Name the problem. Be the solution.
**Framework:** "Based on [what I've learned about your team/challenge], I believe my experience in [specific skill] would help because [direct connection]."

### Slide 6
**Headline:** "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
**Body:** They're checking if you'll stick around and if you're ambitious. Show growth that aligns with the company's trajectory. Don't say "in your chair."
**Framework:** "I want to deepen my expertise in [relevant area] and eventually [realistic growth path]. What excites me about [company] is [specific growth opportunity here]."

### Slide 7
**Headline:** "Why are you leaving your current job?"
**Body:** Never trash your current employer. Focus on what you're moving TOWARD, not what you're running FROM.
**Framework:** "I've learned a lot at [current company], especially [genuine positive]. I'm looking for [specific thing this new role offers] -- that's what drew me to this opportunity."

### Slide 8
**Headline:** The Pattern You Should Notice
**Body:** Every strong answer has 3 things: Specificity (real details, not vague claims). Structure (STAR method keeps you focused). Connection (tie YOUR story to THEIR needs). Generic answers get generic rejections.

### Slide 9
**Headline:** The Difference Between Knowing and Doing
**Body:** Reading these slides is step one. But interviews are a performance. You need reps. That's why people freeze up -- they studied the answers but never practiced saying them out loud.

## CAROUSEL 3: "The Nursing Interview Prep Sheet"

**Format:** 9 slides, 1080x1350px portrait
**Target:** Nursing students, new grad nurses, nurses switching specialties
**Caption:** Nursing interviews are different. You need clinical examples, SBAR structure, and the ability to show patient-first thinking under pressure. Here's your prep sheet -- built with real nurse educators. Save this before your next panel interview.
**Hashtags:** #nursinginterview #nursingschool #newgradnurse #nursingstudent #nurselife #sbar #nursingcareer #nurseinterviewpro #nclex2026

### Slide 1 (Cover)
**Headline:** The Nursing Interview Prep Sheet
**Subtext:** SBAR-structured answers for the questions they always ask
**Design note:** Teal + white, stethoscope icon, clean medical aesthetic

### Slide 2
**Headline:** "Tell me about a time you handled a difficult patient"
**SBAR Framework:**
- **S (Situation):** Set the scene -- unit, shift, patient context
- **B (Background):** Relevant history -- what led to the difficulty
- **A (Assessment):** What you noticed and clinically reasoned
- **R (Recommendation):** What you did and the patient outcome
**Key tip:** Focus on YOUR communication and critical thinking, not the patient's behavior.

### Slide 3
**Headline:** "How do you prioritize when you have multiple critical patients?"
**Answer framework:** Show your clinical reasoning process. Name the framework you use (ABCs, Maslow's hierarchy, acuity level). Give a specific shift example. Interviewers want to see that you have a SYSTEM, not that you wing it.

### Slide 4
**Headline:** "Describe a time you made a medication error or near-miss"
**Why they ask it:** They want to know you're honest, you follow reporting protocols, and you learn from mistakes.
**Answer structure:** What happened (briefly) + What you did immediately (reported, assessed patient) + What changed in your practice + What you'd tell a new nurse about it.
**Never:** Blame the system without owning your part.

### Slide 5
**Headline:** "How do you handle conflict with a physician?"
**What they want to hear:** That you advocate for patients while maintaining professional relationships. Use SBAR to frame the conversation you had.
**Red flag answer:** "I just do what the doctor says." That's a patient safety concern, not a strength.
**Green flag answer:** Shows you communicated concerns clearly, used appropriate channels, and focused on patient outcomes.

### Slide 6
**Headline:** "Why this unit / specialty / hospital?"
**What they want:** Genuine interest, not a generic answer. Research the unit's patient population, their Magnet status, recent initiatives, or culture.
**Framework:** "I'm drawn to [specialty] because [personal clinical experience]. What stands out about [this hospital/unit] is [specific thing you researched]."

### Slide 7
**Headline:** "What would you do if you disagreed with a care plan?"
**Strong answer structure:** Acknowledge the plan + State your clinical concern with evidence + Describe how you'd communicate it (SBAR to charge nurse/provider) + Show willingness to escalate through proper chain if needed.
**The point:** Patient advocacy and professional communication. That's it.

### Slide 8
**Headline:** 3 Things Every Nursing Interviewer Looks For
**Body:**
1. **Clinical judgment** -- Can you think through a situation, not just follow orders?
2. **Communication** -- Can you articulate your reasoning clearly under pressure?
3. **Patient-first mindset** -- Does every answer come back to the patient?
If your answer doesn't demonstrate at least one of these, restructure it.

**Headline:** Practice Nursing Interviews With AI Built By Nurses
**Body:** NurseInterviewPro uses questions written by real nurse educators, SBAR-structured feedback, and AI coaching designed specifically for clinical interviews. Your first practice session is free.
**CTA:** Start free at NurseInterviewPro.ai
**Design note:** Teal gradient, nursing-specific imagery

# SECTION 7: CREATIVE BRIEF NOTES

## Brand Colors
- **Primary:** Teal (#0D9488 or similar)
- **Secondary:** Navy/dark (#1E293B)
- **Accent:** White
- **Avoid:** Red, bright yellow, anything that feels like a hospital

## Typography
- Headlines: Bold sans-serif (Inter, Satoshi, or similar)
- Body: Clean, readable
- On-screen text: Large enough for mobile, high contrast

## Tone Reminders
- We understand anxiety is real. Don't minimize it.
- Never "just be confident" -- give tools and frameworks instead.
- Slightly edgy but never mean. We're rooting for the viewer.
- For nursing content: respect the profession. These people save lives. The interview is just one obstacle.
- Always position the app as a practice tool, not a replacement for real preparation.

## CTAs That Work
- "Free to start" (reduces friction)
- "No credit card needed" (trust signal)
- "Link in bio" / "Link below" (platform-appropriate)
- "Try it before your next interview" (urgency without pressure)
- Avoid: "Sign up now!" "Limited time!" "You NEED this!" (too salesy)
