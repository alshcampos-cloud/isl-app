Section 02

What Career Services Is Trying to Achieve

Jessica Li serves all 8,913 USF students. She asked about group pricing — which means she's thinking about reach. But the reason she's reaching out tells us about her motives. Here's what Career Services likely cares about — and how each option maps to those goals.

🎯

Fill a Known Gap

USF already pays for Big Interview (general interview prep). They have nothing for nursing. With ~840 nursing students entering the most competitive healthcare job market in a decade, this is a gap Career Services can see clearly.

"Our nursing students don't have a dedicated tool. They need interview prep specific to their field."
🎓

Invest in Student Skill Development

Some institutions buy tools. Others invest in skill development as a program. If USF sees interview preparation as a core part of producing career-ready graduates — not just a one-off resource — they'll want broader coverage and longer access.

"We want to build professional communication skills into the student experience, not just hand out passes."
📈

Demonstrate ROI to Leadership

Career Services reports to the VP of Student Affairs. They need engagement data: how many students signed up, how often they practiced, what features they used. Usage reporting turns a purchase into a measurable initiative they can defend in budget reviews.

"I need data to show this is working when my VP asks about next year's budget."

Reach: Who Does Jessica Want to Impact?

This is the key question that determines which option fits. It's not just about budget — it's about how wide Career Services wants to cast the net.

Reach Goal Who Gets Access Students Maps To
Targeted — Students who need it most BSN seniors + graduating MSN students ~400 Option A
Comprehensive — Every nursing student covered All nursing students (BSN juniors/seniors + MSN + DNP) ~850 Option B

The Skill Development Spectrum

Option A is a tool purchase. Option B is a skill development investment. Option C is an institutional career readiness initiative. The higher the tier, the more USF is signaling that interview preparation and professional communication are core to the student experience — not just a nice-to-have for graduating seniors.

Section 05

Why No Free Trial

We Don't Offer Free Trials for Institutional Partnerships

Here's why — and what we offer instead.
  1. The product speaks for itself. We offer a live demo on the call. Career Services sees exactly what students get — no guesswork.
  2. A free trial devalues the tool in the eyes of Career Services and students. If it's free, it's perceived as optional.
  3. Students perform better when they know the institution invested in them. Institutional buy-in signals "this matters."
  4. Career Services can evaluate the product via demo + usage reporting after the first month. Data, not guesses.
  5. Our pricing is already competitive — $4.48–$12.50/student vs. $19.99 individual retail. The discount is the incentive.
  6. Instead of a trial, we offer a satisfaction guarantee.
Our Guarantee

If usage is below expectations after the first semester, we adjust the next semester's pricing. We stand behind the product with data, not freebies.

Alumni Risk USF alumni keep @usfca.edu emails indefinitely. Recent nursing grads actively job-hunting = heaviest users. With email domain gating, we'd pay API costs for users USF isn't paying for. This creates uncapped cost exposure.

Bottom Line — Read Jessica's Motives, Then Recommend

It Depends on What USF Wants to Achieve

The right option isn't about budget alone. It's about what Career Services is trying to accomplish and how far they want their reach to extend. Here's how we read the call and respond:

If Jessica says: "We need something for nursing students"

Lead with Option A ($4,999). She's filling a gap. Fast approval, under $5K. Get us in the door. Prove value, expand later.

Motive: Fill a gap. Reach: Targeted.

If Jessica says: "We want to invest in our nursing students' career readiness"

Lead with Option B ($7,999). She sees this as skill development. Full coverage, monthly reporting, feature requests. This is a partnership, not a purchase.

Motive: Skill development. Reach: Comprehensive.

If Jessica says: "I serve all 8,913 students"

Present Option C ($14,999). She's thinking campus-wide. Both products, all disciplines, priority everything. Position as institutional career readiness initiative.

Motive: Institutional initiative. Reach: Campus-wide.

The Question for the Call

The single most important question to ask Jessica on the demo call: "Are you looking to cover specific student groups, or are you interested in making interview preparation a broader part of USF's career readiness offerings?"

Her answer tells us which option to present first. Then we anchor, and let her negotiate toward the tier that matches her budget and goals.
