## Key numbers Jacob should know going in

(Pulled from `USF_Partnership_Analysis.md` — read that doc for full citations)

- **USF total enrollment:** 8,913 students. Nursing-specific: ~1,095 (1,508 SONHP students total)
- **Anchor offer:** $4,999/year flat institutional license for NurseInterviewPro (nursing students)
- **Add-on offer:** InterviewAnswers.AI for all 8,913 students
- **Bundle:** $7,999/year for both
- **Pilot opener:** free pilot semester to lower friction
- **Why $4,999/$7,999 and not more:** USF's direct-purchase threshold is $5,000 — under that, Senior Director Alex Hochman can buy without formal procurement. Above it, full RFP cycle.
- **Decision-makers:** Jessica (counselor, gathers info) → Alex Hochman (budget authority, ahochman@usfca.edu) → Ellen Kelly (program decisions, kellye@usfca.edu)
- **Competitive frame:** USF already pays Big Interview. We're not creating a new budget category — we're replacing or augmenting an existing one.

## How to adapt this for a non-USF school

The `USF_Proposal_External.html` variant has the most generic framing — easiest starting point if the school Jacob is talking to isn't USF. Edit-in-place to swap:
- School name + enrollment numbers (replace USF's 8,913 with the new school's count)
- Decision-maker org chart (USF reports through Student Life → VP Student Affairs → Provost)
- The $5K direct-purchase threshold may be different at a public university (likely lower)
- Bundle pricing scales with enrollment — for a 20K+ student school, the bundle anchor should likely lift to $9,999 or $11,999

Strategic principles stay the same:
1. Anchor under the school's direct-purchase threshold
2. Pilot semester to lower friction
3. Frame as replacing/augmenting an existing budget category (not new budget)
4. Lead with nursing if the school has a strong nursing program
