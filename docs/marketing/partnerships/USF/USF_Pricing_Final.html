Part 1

What We Found: Errors, Assumptions & Corrections

Over the course of building four pricing documents, we identified several errors and false assumptions that skewed our numbers. All are now corrected.

Errors Identified

Error 1 — Critical The Double Discount Problem

Our semester pricing stacked two discounts: a volume discount on the monthly rate, PLUS treating that discounted 1-month price as the entire 4-month semester cost.

Old (Wrong)

840 students × $9.52/student = $7,999/semester

$9.52 is the monthly rate. We charged 1 month for 4 months of access.

Effective monthly cost to USF: $2.38/student. That's 88% off.

Corrected

840 × ($9.52/mo × 2.5 months) = $19,992/semester

Semester = discounted monthly rate × 2.5 active months.

Effective monthly cost to USF: $5.95/student. That's 70% off — fair.

Error 2 Apples-to-Oranges Savings Claims

We claimed "85-93% off" by comparing a semester institutional price against the monthly retail price ($19.99). The correct comparison is semester vs. semester: institutional price vs. $79.96 (4 × $19.99). Even when corrected, the old discounts were 85-88% — far too generous.

Error 3 Inconsistent Cohort Numbers

Some documents used 850 nursing students, others used 840. Actual total addressable: ~840 (250 BSN Seniors + 200 ME-MSN + 90 DNP + 250 BSN Juniors + 50 RN-to-MSN). Per-student math: $7,999 ÷ 840 = $9.52, not $9.41 as shown in some charts.

Error 4 Two Models With Contradictory Prices

ERIN_Pricing_Review.md proposed flat licenses ($2,499/semester for ~500-800 nursing codes). USF_Pricing_Charts.html used per-student tiered pricing ($9.52/student × 840 = $7,999). These are fundamentally different: the flat model = $3.12-$5.00/student, the per-student model = $9.52/student. With the ×2.5 correction, the gap widens further. We needed to pick one coherent approach.

Assumptions Corrected

Assumption Corrected $16K Procurement Threshold Was Made Up

We asserted that anything above $16K "changes the conversation" for USF. This was entirely subjective with zero data basis. The only verified threshold: $5,000 (under = Senior Director can approve directly). Above $5K, we don't know the process or difficulty. We should price for our business, not guess at their budget.

Assumption Corrected No Stripe Fees on Institutional Deals

Previous models included Stripe fees ($0.47-$0.88/pass). Institutional deals are invoiced via purchase order/check — no per-transaction processing. This improves our margins on every institutional pass.

Key Insight Students Who Pivot Across Roles Drive Higher Usage

A nursing student might interview for ICU, get rejected, then try ED or med-surg months later. Or a student might explore general interview skills across different roles during a semester. This means semester students may use more than a typical 30-day retail consumer, not less. The ×2.5 multiplier accounts for this without overcorrecting.

Why ×2.5, Not ×1, ×2, or ×4?

×1 (old model): Charges for 1 month, gives 4. Leaves 75% of access time unmonetized. Too generous.
×2: Generous. Doesn't account for the nurse who fails one interview and preps again for another role.
×2.5: Realistic. Students prep in bursts (2-3 weeks before each interview), with gaps for exams, clinicals, and jobs. Accounts for 1-2 prep cycles. Our recommended multiplier.
×3: Conservative. Fair if we expect heavier usage. Slightly penalizes light users.
×4: No time discount at all — full pay for full access. Too expensive for an institutional pitch.

Key Cost Fact: No Stripe Fees on Institutional Deals

Institutional contracts are invoiced via purchase order/check. No 2.9% + $0.30 per-transaction Stripe fee. This saves $0.47-$0.88 per pass compared to retail, and improves margins by 2-5 percentage points across every tier.

Pricing Methodology Internal Only

How we arrive at our per-student semester prices. This is our internal math — never show to USF.

1
Start with retail monthly price: $19.99/month (nursing)

This is the anchor. Everything is a discount from here.

2
Apply volume discount (more students = lower monthly rate)
Volume Discount Monthly Rate Rationale
250 students 30% $13.99/mo Entry-level institutional discount
450 students 40% $11.99/mo Core graduating cohorts
540 students 44% $11.11/mo + advanced practice
840 students 52% $9.52/mo Full nursing program
3
For semester passes: multiply by 2.5 active months

Students access for 4 months but actively use ~2.5 months. Accounts for exam gaps, clinical rotations, and role-pivoting students.

4
Verify: per-student semester vs. retail semester

Retail semester = 4 × $19.99 = $79.96. Our institutional semester price should be 50-70% below this — a meaningful discount that's still economically sound.

5
Verify margins: institutional price minus our cost

No Stripe fees on institutional deals. Our cost is API only ($3.10/user/semester avg, $11.53 worst-case power user). Target: 85%+ margin at average usage.

On the Call

  1. Demo the product (nursing mock interview, SBAR coaching, practice mode)
  2. Ask: "Which students are you most focused on covering?"
  3. Present 2-3 options based on her answer (don't show all options — tailor)
  4. Anchor at the appropriate option (if she says "graduating students" → Option 2 at $13,491)
  5. Always offer the free pilot as a fallback: "Want to start with a small group first?"
  6. If they ask about general: "We can bundle both — let me show you what that looks like"
