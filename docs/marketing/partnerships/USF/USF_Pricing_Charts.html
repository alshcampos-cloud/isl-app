Pass Duration

Why Semester Passes Make More Sense for Students

Students aren't typical consumers. They have competing demands — classes, exams, clinical rotations, part-time jobs — that limit when they can actively practice. A 30-day window is often too short because real life gets in the way.

The Student Reality
A 30-day pass means:
  • Midterm week = 1 week lost (25% of the pass)
  • Clinical rotation schedules conflict
  • Part-time job shifts eat into practice time
  • If they need to pivot to a new opportunity, they need a second pass
A semester pass means:
  • Students use it when their schedule allows
  • Exam weeks aren't wasted time
  • Can practice early, revisit before interviews
  • One pass covers all opportunities that semester
Lower Usage Overhead for Institutional Clients

Because students spread their usage across a semester with natural gaps (exams, holidays, rotations), the actual API cost per institutional student is lower than our average consumer user. Our $3.10/user/semester estimate is a ceiling — real institutional cost is likely closer to $2.00–$2.50/user/semester. This means our margins improve further on institutional deals.

Our Recommendation for Institutions: Semester Passes

For institutional partners, we recommend semester passes as the default. Students aren't power users on a 30-day sprint — they're learners building a skill around a busy schedule. Semester access respects the reality of student life and gives Career Services a full picture of engagement. 30-day passes are available for targeted, short-term needs, but semester is where the value is for both sides.

Before We Recommend

A Few Questions to Find the Right Fit

The right configuration depends on your goals. These questions help us recommend the best setup.

Question 1
What's the primary goal?
Is this about giving students prolonged access to build interview skills over time? Or focused prep right before they're actively interviewing? This determines 30-day vs. semester passes.
Question 2
Who are you looking to cover?
Mainly graduating nursing students entering the workforce? All nursing years? General students across other programs too? This determines volume and which products.
Question 3
How would you distribute?
Would you want specific codes to hand out to individual students? Broader access for an entire cohort? We're building an access code system where each code activates one pass — your team controls distribution.
Question 4
Nursing only, or both products?
Some nursing students benefit from general interview prep too (behavioral questions, STAR method). And Career Services may want to offer general prep across other schools within USF.

Student ID Verification

Access codes alone don't verify that someone is an active, enrolled student. We need to ensure institutional passes go to current students — not alumni, not former students, not shared accounts.

Why This Matters

USF alumni retain @usfca.edu emails indefinitely. Recent nursing graduates are the most likely to use interview prep tools — they're actively job-hunting. Without verification, institutional passes could be used by people USF isn't paying for, creating uncapped cost exposure for us and unfair distribution for USF.

Verification Approach How It Works Blocks Alumni?
Access code + Student ID number Student enters both their access code AND student ID at signup. Career Services provides us a list of valid IDs to check against. Yes
Career Services controlled distribution Codes are only distributed to enrolled students through official channels (Canvas, advising, orientation). Semester expiration limits exposure. Mostly
Third-party verification (SheerID, etc.) External service verifies enrollment status in real time. Adds $1–2/verification to our cost. Yes
Our Plan

We want to work with Career Services to determine the right verification approach for USF. The simplest path: access code + student ID number validation at signup. Career Services provides a roster of active student IDs each semester, and our system checks against it during code redemption. This ensures every pass goes to a current, enrolled student.
